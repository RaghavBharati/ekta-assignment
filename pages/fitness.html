Movement made simple for busy lives — small sessions that fit between work and life and leave you sharper, calmer and stronger.

Why move?

Exercise is daily fuel, not a chore

You don't need a gym membership or two spare hours a day. A few short bursts of movement each week can transform how you feel, think and cope with a heavy schedule.

When you move, your body releases endorphins and boosts blood flow to the brain — which is why a ten-minute walk before a big task often clears your head faster than another coffee. Regular activity has been shown to lift mood, steady energy and help you sleep, all of which stack the odds in favour of better days.

  • More energy: beat the mid-afternoon slump without another energy drink.
  • Sharper focus: movement improves memory and concentration when you need to focus.
  • Less stress: a natural release valve for deadline pressure and anxiety.
  • Better sleep: tire your body the healthy way and wake up ready.

Move library

Beginner exercises & form tips

Master these six fundamentals and you can build almost any workout. Focus on control, not speed.

Squats

Feet shoulder-width apart, sit back as if reaching for a chair. Keep your chest up and knees tracking over your toes — never let them cave inward.

Push-ups

Hands under shoulders, body in one straight line. Lower with elbows at roughly 45°. Too tough? Drop to your knees and keep the plank shape.

Plank

Forearms down, squeeze your core and glutes so hips don't sag. Aim for quality seconds, not a saggy minute. Breathe steadily throughout.

Lunges

Step forward and lower until both knees hit about 90°. Keep your torso tall and your front knee behind your toes. Alternate legs each rep.

Glute Bridge

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive through your heels to lift hips, squeeze at the top, then lower with control. Great for desk-tight hips.

Jumping Jacks

A classic full-body warm-up. Land softly with bent knees to protect your joints, and keep a steady rhythm to raise your heart rate quickly.
